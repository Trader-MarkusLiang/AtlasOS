# Price Transmission

Price Transmission tracks whether upstream constraints can pass through into revenue, margin, and
earnings.

| Chain | Upstream Change | Transmission Path | Beneficiary | Watch Item |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Memory | HBM / DRAM demand tightens supply | Price and mix improve revenue and margins if capacity remains disciplined | DRAM / HBM leaders and memory supply chain | Pricing power, inventory, capex discipline, FCF |
| Equipment | Memory and foundry capacity expansion continues | More process steps and fab investment support orders/backlog | Etch, deposition, cleaning, CMP, lithography ecosystem | Order certainty, backlog conversion, customer capex |
| Materials | More advanced process and capacity expansion raise materials intensity | Consumables and specialty materials gain volume and pricing leverage | Targets, precursors, CMP materials, quartz, wafers, chemicals | Qualification status, customer concentration, margin pass-through |
| Bandwidth | AI interconnect and optical demand rise | Higher-speed optical modules, fiber, and CPO demand transmit into revenue | Optical module, fiber, optical interconnect chain | Customer adoption, pricing, inventory, domestic substitution |

## Transmission Rule

Do not treat upstream tightness as earnings impact until the path reaches at least one of:

1. Revenue growth.
2. Margin expansion.
3. Backlog conversion.
4. Capex acceleration.
5. FCF improvement or deterioration that changes ROI.
